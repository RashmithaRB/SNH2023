Alluvial Soil

Alluvial SoilAlluvial soil is one of the most fertile types of soil and is formed by the deposition of sediments carried by rivers, streams, and other water bodies. Alluvial soil is highly fertile due to its rich mineral content, especially in terms of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. This makes it ideal for agriculture.

Black Soil

Black SoilBlack soil, also known as black cotton soil or regur soil, is a type of soil renowned for its fertility and unique characteristics. Its distinct dark color, often black or deep shades of brown, gives it the name "black soil." This color is due to the high content of organic matter and minerals like iron.

Laterite Soil

Laterite SoilLaterite soil is a type of soil that develops in tropical and subtropical regions with high temperatures and heavy rainfall. Initially, laterite soil appears reddish due to the presence of iron oxides, giving it a rusty or reddish hue. The texture of laterite soil can range from gravelly to clayey.
